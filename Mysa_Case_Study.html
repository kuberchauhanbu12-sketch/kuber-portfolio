⚡ TL;DR

💡

The problem: Mysa currently requires every customer to book a demo, speak to sales, and go through a 2-week setup before seeing any value. For tech founders — Mysa's fastest-converting segment — this 17-day gap is where they lose interest and go back to Excel.

🎯

The solution: A self-serve Product-Led Growth (PLG) onboarding that shows a tech founder their exact burn rate in under 10 minutes — before asking them to complete any setup — so value comes before effort, not after it.

📌

Honest caveat: This is a validated hypothesis based on Mysa's public product and customer data. What happens inside their demo call is not publicly available. The prototype assumes that gap exists and should be confirmed with 3 customer interviews before building.

🔍 The Problem — Based on Real Data

⚠️ Hypothesis — validated by public data, not internal metrics

After studying Mysa's public product, demo flow, and customer stories, I found one structural gap that likely costs them conversions every month:

🚫

Mysa has no self-serve path. Every customer must book a demo, speak to a salesperson, then complete a 2-week setup before seeing any value. There is no "sign up and try" option. This is confirmed directly from mysa.io — the only CTA is "Book a Demo."

The evidence from Mysa's own website:

Observation Source Implication
No free trial button on homepage mysa.io Zero self-serve entry point
"Complete implementation in 2 weeks" mysa.io/solution/tech-startups 17+ days from interest to first value
Demo form filters under 100 bills/month mysa.io/book-a-demo Confirms ICP but also shows high friction entry
Drink Prime started with zero balance, 1 account mysa.io/customers/drink-prime Trust ladder exists but is not designed — customers figure it out alone

Why this matters for conversion:

A tech founder discovers Mysa on Monday. They are interested. They book a demo. Demo is scheduled for Wednesday. Setup starts Thursday. Two weeks later the trial ends. In those 17 days: three customer escalations, two investor calls, one hiring crisis. Setup kept getting pushed. They never finished. Trial ends. They go back to Excel. Not because Mysa is bad — because something more urgent always won.

💡

The core insight: The problem is not that setup is hard. The problem is that Mysa asks for effort before delivering value. Flip the sequence — show value in 10 minutes, let full setup happen naturally after — and conversion changes fundamentally.

Market context:

  • India has ~207,000 DPIIT-recognised startups (January 2026)
  • Tech startups: ~25% = ~52,000 companies
  • With 100+ bills/month and active ERP: ~5,000-6,000 companies (Mysa's realistic TAM)
  • Estimated Mysa customers today: 100-300 companies (under 5% of TAM)
  • Runway to grow: massive — but high-touch sales model cannot scale to thousands

🛠️ The Solution — Product-Led Growth Onboarding

Instead of demo → sales call → 2-week setup → first value, design a self-serve path that delivers first value in 10 minutes, then lets full setup happen naturally.

The principle: Value before effort. Always.

1
Sign up with Google — zero friction
Name, email, company. No credit card. No sales call. No demo booking. Just start.
⏱ 90 seconds
2
Connect one bank account — read only
Only view access. Zero payment risk. "We cannot make any payments yet. You are in full control." Trust addressed before it becomes a concern.
⏱ 2-3 minutes
3
The wow moment — burn rate in 5 minutes
Mysa instantly shows: burn last month, runway, expense breakdown by category. The number their investor asks for every Monday. Available in one screen. Pulled live. No Excel needed. This is the moment they never forget.
⏱ Under 5 minutes total from signup
4
Process first bill — 90 seconds
Forward any bill to bills@mysa.io. Mysa AI scans it, calculates GST and TDS automatically, maps to right ledger, flags duplicates. What took 20 minutes took 90 seconds. Second wow moment.
⏱ 90 seconds
5
Natural expansion — no pressure
Connect payment account, ERP, invite finance person — when ready. No rush. Burn rate is already live. Each expansion step deepens embedding and raises switching cost naturally.
⏱ Over days/weeks — self-directed
🔑

Why this works for trust: The Drink Prime case study showed that even sophisticated finance leaders start with zero balance and one account. They built their own trust ladder manually. This prototype makes that trust ladder a designed, intentional product experience — not something each customer discovers alone.

📊 How We Measure Success

⭐
North Star Metric
Time to First Value (TTFV)
Minutes from signup to first burn rate seen. Current: 17+ days. Target: Under 10 minutes.
Primary Metric
Trial to Paid Conversion Rate
Target: +30-40% improvement from baseline after PLG launch
Secondary Metric
Onboarding Step Completion Rate
Which step do most founders drop at? Fix that step first.
Secondary Metric
DAU During Trial
Are they coming back daily? If yes — conversion is near. If no — wow moment failed.
Guardrail Metric
Customer Quality Score
Average bills/month of converted customers must stay above 100. Do not dilute ICP while opening self-serve.
📌

Note on metrics: Mysa's actual baseline conversion rate is internal data I do not have access to. These targets are directional, not absolute. The real baseline should be pulled from Mysa's analytics before setting specific percentage targets.

🔬 What I Would Validate Before Building

This prototype is a well-researched hypothesis. Before any engineering investment, I would validate 3 things:

# Question How to validate Time
1 Does Mysa already show burn rate in their demo call? Sit in 3 sales demo calls 1 week
2 Where do trial users actually drop off? Pull funnel data from Mixpanel or internal analytics 2 days
3 Would a tech founder sign up without a demo call? Interview 5 founders who considered Mysa but did not convert 1 week
✅

If validated: Build the PLG onboarding as described. Expected outcome: significant improvement in trial-to-paid conversion for tech startups without increasing sales headcount.

↩️

If not validated: The real drop-off point might be elsewhere — post-payment setup, ERP integration, or team adoption. The same framework of "find where effort exceeds value and flip the sequence" still applies, just at a different stage.
