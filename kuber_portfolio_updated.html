Case Study 01 · B2B SaaS · HireBound
The Signal in the Noise:
HireBound's Commitment Problem
Transitioning HireBound from Sourcing Volume to Commitment Prediction
Core Problem
Low 90-day Retention
Target Metric
High-Intent Response Rate
Projected Uplift
25% → 40–45%
Market
4–5 Lakh roles/month
The Problem

HireBound already achieves a massive ~64% response rate at the top of the funnel. That's not the problem. Hiring keeps failing because candidates say yes — then don't show. Early attrition runs 30–50% within the first 90 days across the industry.

The system is optimising for the wrong thing: response volume instead of commitment quality.

"Hiring doesn't fail due to a lack of candidates — it fails due to a lack of committed candidates."
User Research — The Marketplace Friction Matrix

👩‍💼 Sarah — The Recruiter

  • Goal: Hire reliable candidates who stay past 90 days
  • Pain: Candidates accept then vanish
  • Fear: Held accountable when tools fail her

👨‍💼 Manish — The Candidate

  • Goal: Find a real job, quickly
  • Pain: Unclear if opportunities are genuine
  • Fear: Wasting time on dead-ends

Key insight: The current system optimises for response volume, creating high activity but low mutual trust on both sides of the marketplace.

Root Cause — The Flawed Funnel

The funnel prioritises skill-based qualification early, entirely bypassing expectation alignment. This produces false positives — candidates who look qualified but aren't actually committed to joining or staying.

The result: a low-intent pipeline that creates high downstream drop-off, wasted recruiter time, and eroded client trust.

"Hiring is not a matching problem. It is a commitment prediction problem."
The Solution — Intent & Fit Qualification Layer

A lightweight 3-stage pre-qualification system inserted before skill matching. Fast, low-friction, filters for commitment first.

Stage 1
Intent Check
10–15 sec · Actively looking? Sales-specific? Join date?
→
Stage 2
Fit & Expectation
20–30 sec · 6 days/week? Salary range? Relocation?
→
Stage 3
Skill Qualification
Existing system — now applied only to high-intent fits
→
Output
Committed Hires
Intent Score + Fit Score validated

Plus: a Candidate Memory Layer using event-driven architecture — stores intent, preferences, and baseline expectations. Reuses data in future interactions instead of re-asking. Shifts the core question from "Can they do the job?" → "Will they do the job and stay?"

The next leap for HireBound: From an automation tool for sourcing → a decision engine for reliable hiring.

Case Study 02 · Consumer App · EdTech
The Guided Choice Strategy:
Duolingo's Day 7 Retention
Addressing the Habit Death Zone through Guided Choice Architecture
Primary Metric
Day 7 Retention
Target
+20% in 6 months
Target Segment
18–24 year-olds
Test Design
50/50 A/B Test · 2–4 weeks
Why Day 7 Is the Only Metric That Matters

Day 7 retention is the absolute prerequisite for Lifetime Value (LTV) in a freemium model. The retention engine drives everything: Early habit formation (Days 1–7) → Day 7 retention → Long-term engagement → Subscription conversion (5–8%) AND maximised ad impressions per user.

Miss Day 7 and users don't come back. The business impact is direct and compounding.

The Habit Death Zone — Diagnosing the Drop

The 18–24 segment — Duolingo's largest, most gamification-responsive base — drops from 100% to approximately 15% retention by Day 7. They arrive with high curiosity and fail to form a consistent habit before the window closes.

System Reality

  • Fixed, strict linear learning path
  • Highly predictable early lessons
  • Competitive elements locked behind progression
  • Weak trigger-action-reward cycle

User Feeling

  • Lack of control and autonomy
  • Repetitive, monotonous early experience
  • Work before play — delayed time to fun
  • Inconsistent motivation to return
"Our greatest strength — structured learning — is causing our greatest weakness — early churn. We must introduce controlled agency to repair the onboarding experience."
Core Conflict — The Autonomy-Structure Balance

The design space runs from Total Chaos (complete autonomy, high motivation, low educational effectiveness) to Rigid Dictation (current state — forced linear, high educational effectiveness, high churn). The optimal synthesis: Guided Choice — freedom within boundaries.

The Solution — The Guided Choice Layer

On each login during Days 1–7, instead of a forced lesson, the user makes a deliberate selection from 3–4 capped options. The architecture introduces flexibility at the point of action — but guarantees convergence back to core retention mechanics (XP + streak).

⚡ Quick Challenge

High-energy bursts + competitive fun. Available Day 1. Eliminates the "work before play" problem immediately.

📚 Practice Basics

Core structured learning — chosen voluntarily. The mandatory long-term path, now a deliberate selection.

🎧 Listening Mode

Reduces cognitive load. Ideal for commuting. Keeps streak alive on low-energy days without breaking the habit.

🛡️ Streak Saver

Low-friction safety net for high-fatigue days. Capped via dynamic difficulty thresholds to prevent over-reliance.

Guardrail design: Easy modes (Streak Saver) frequency-limited and dynamically capped. Core learning path remains mandatory beyond Day 7. Max 3–4 options to prevent decision fatigue.

Experiment Design & Success Metrics

Structure: 50/50 A/B split. Group A: current linear experience. Group B: choice-based experience. Duration: 2–4 weeks. Constraint: leverage existing gamification infrastructure; no paid conversion pushes during test window.

The Compounding Cost of Cancellations

Night cancellations aren't just a UX issue — they trigger a negative feedback doom loop. A cancelled ride creates: driver inefficiency (wasted fuel, time) → poor rider experience (extended wait, anxiety) → platform trust erosion → user migration to competitors → revenue loss → fewer drivers incentivised to work nights → more cancellations.

Opportunity sizing: 1.3M estimated daily rides in India. 40% occur at night (520K). 25% cancellation rate = 130K cancellations/night. A 30% reduction saves 39,000 rides every single night.

Two-sided User Research

🚗 Demand Side — Riders

  • Ages 18–30: price-sensitive, switches apps instantly if delayed
  • Ages 31–60: values reliability above all else
  • Core expectation: fast pickups, transparency, safety

🚙 Supply Side — Drivers

  • New (0–2y): motivated by incentives, generally accepts most rides
  • Experienced (3–5y): highly selective, avoids long or unprofitable pickups
  • Core motivation: profitability, predictable routes, safety

Strategic conclusion: Drivers hold ultimate veto power over every transaction. They decide whether to accept, ignore, or cancel. Focus must be on supply-side confidence first.

Root Cause — Information Asymmetry
"Most cancellations occur because neither side has the full picture. Uncertainty breeds cancellations."

Driver Blind Spots

  • Exact drop location and destination
  • Route convenience and traffic conditions
  • Immediate trip profitability

Rider Blind Spots

  • Realistic expected wait times
  • True ride confirmation status
  • Driver commitment level to pickup
The 2-Pillar Solution Framework

🔵 Pillar 1: Empowering Supply

  • Directional Drop-offs: Show distance range + rough direction (e.g. "Northwest, 8–12km") before acceptance — cures route anxiety without revealing rider's exact address
  • Night Surge Incentives: Targeted bonus payouts during night hours — compensates for lower volume and higher perceived risk, fundamentally improving driver motivation

🟠 Pillar 2: Reassuring Demand

  • Accurate Wait Timers: Real-time arrival clocks proactively reduce rider anxiety and app-switching behaviour
  • Smart Reassignment: If driver rejects, system instantly triggers background re-routing — friction masked from rider entirely
  • Booking Transparency: Clear UI states (Searching → Accepted → Arriving) keep the user anchored and builds platform confidence
